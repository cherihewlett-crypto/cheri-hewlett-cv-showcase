# Workflow Automation Case Study

## Summary

Designed automation patterns that connect planning, execution, and review across systems like Notion, Supabase, GitHub, and AI tooling.

## Context

Operational work often breaks down at handoffs:

- issues are raised without enough structure
- priorities drift
- actions lose ownership
- completion gets reported without proof

I focus on automation that improves flow **and** preserves governance.

## Approach

The workflow pattern emphasizes:

- clear intake structure
- explicit priority and escalation logic
- durable system-of-record data
- authenticated completion signals
- traceable movement from idea to queue to resolution

## Representative outcomes

- stronger issue hygiene
- less manual coordination across tools
- better visibility into what is pending, what is blocked, and what is truly complete
- improved confidence in cross-functional operating cadence

## Public-safe proof

- Accessible platform materials show queue design with priority, escalation, GitHub linkage, acceptance criteria, and verification timestamps.
- Public-safe implementation patterns also include authenticated completion handling and protection against premature "done" states.

## Role

Platform and workflow architect.

## Boundaries

- This case study reflects workflow architecture and governance design.
- It avoids proprietary customer specifics and internal-only operating details.
